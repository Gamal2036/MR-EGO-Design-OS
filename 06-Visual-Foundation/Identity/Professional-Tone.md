# Professional Tone

**Phase:** DP-5 (Visual Foundation)
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** FOUNDATION — Permanent
**Design Authority:** DP-0 ([Brand-Constitution.md](../../01-Constitution/Brand-Constitution.md), [Design-Principles.md](../../01-Constitution/Design-Principles.md))

---

## Definition

Professional Tone is the visual equivalent of brand voice. It is how the interface presents itself — the visual manners, etiquette, and comportment of every surface, control, and transition.

---

## Tone Dimensions

### Authoritative Without Arrogance

The interface leads with confidence but never lectures.

- Headings are bold but not oversized
- Primary actions are prominent but not aggressive
- AI suggestions are clear but presented as options, not commands
- Error messages explain without condescension

### Warm Without Informality

Professional warmth is appropriate for a career platform — respectful, kind, and human.

- Rounded corners are 6px, not 20px (pill shapes imply consumer app)
- Illustrations are abstract, not cartoonish
- Empty states are encouraging, not apologetic
- Success states are satisfying, not celebratory

### Precise Without Pedantry

Every visual detail is intentional but the user never feels the design trying too hard.

- Animation durations are tuned for feel, not show
- Spacing is generous but not excessive
- Color is deliberate but not loud
- Feedback is immediate but not demanding

### Calm Without Slowness

The interface moves at a composed pace that signals readiness, not lethargy.

- Transitions are 200-300ms — fast enough to feel instant, slow enough to perceive
- Loading states are informative, not urgent
- Notifications arrive quietly, never as interruptions
- AI thinking states communicate progress without hurry

---

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Visual Expression |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| Welcome / Onboarding | Warm + Encouraging | Inviting illustration, generous spacing, soft shadows |
| Dashboard | Calm + Productive | Clean grids, restrained color, clear hierarchy |
| Decision Support | Precise + Neutral | Balanced layouts, evidence presentation, comparative views |
| AI Interaction | Deferential + Clear | Distinctive AI styling, confidence badges, option presentation |
| Error / Warning | Serious + Helpful | Clear iconography, solution-oriented layout, calm color |
| Success | Satisfied + Measured | Checkmark animation, subtle highlight, no confetti |
| Settings | Transparent + Organized | Clear toggles, categorized sections, visible controls |
| Delete / Destroy | Firm + Clear | Red highlights, explicit confirmation, prominent cancel |
| Loading / Waiting | Patient + Informative | Skeleton screens, progress indication, no spinners alone |

---

## Tone Rules

1. **Never sound or look panicked.** Even error states maintain composure.
2. **Never look incomplete.** Every state is designed — loading, empty, error, success.
3. **Never look cheap.** Every surface uses quality visual materials (proper shadows, correct spacing, right colors).
4. **Never look loud.** Color is restrained. Motion is subtle. Contrast is comfortable.
5. **Never look confusing.** Hierarchy is clear. Purpose is obvious. Action is apparent.
6. **Never look dated.** Design decisions follow current standards without chasing trends.
