# Glossary

**Phase:** DP-0 (Foundation)
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** FOUNDATION — Permanent
**Authority:** Definitive reference for all MR:EGO terminology.

---

## A

**Accessibility**
Design practice ensuring that all users, regardless of ability, can perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with the platform. Non-negotiable requirement across all MR:EGO interfaces. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 6.*

**AI Framework**
Shared infrastructure providing AI capabilities (inference, recommendations, natural language processing, pattern detection) to all modules through consistent interfaces. *See [Product-Constitution.md](Product-Constitution.md) — AI Integration Rules.*

**Approval**
The human confirmation step required before MR:EGO executes consequential actions. The "A" in the Guardian-Provider-Execution-Agent model. *See [Project-Constitution.md](Project-Constitution.md) — Human Approval Philosophy.*

**Architecture**
The structural organization of MR:EGO's systems, modules, data flows, and interfaces. Defined in DP-0 and implemented across all subsequent phases. *See [Architecture-Overview.md](Architecture-Overview.md).*

## C

**Career**
The longitudinal representation of a user's professional journey, including employment history, skills, education, projects, achievements, and aspirations. A core domain entity in the MR:EGO data model.

**Career Timeline**
A visual representation of a user's professional history and planned trajectory, combining past experiences, current position, and future goals in a single interactive view.

**Component**
A reusable, self-contained UI element with defined behavior, styling, and API. The atomic unit of the Design System (DP-2). *See [Design-Principles.md](Design-Principles.md).*

**Constitution**
The collective set of permanent governing documents established in DP-0: Project, Brand, Product, and UX Constitutions plus Design Principles. The highest design authority in MR:EGO.

**CRM (Professional Network Module)**
A module for managing professional relationships, tracking interactions, and deriving network insights. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md) — Phase 6.*

## D

**Dashboard**
A customizable overview screen presenting key information, metrics, and actionable items from one or more modules. Governed by UX Rule 4: No Dashboard Clutter. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 4.*

**Data Layer**
The unified data storage, access, and synchronization system shared by all modules. Provides consistent querying, caching, offline support, and data integrity guarantees. *See [Architecture-Overview.md](Architecture-Overview.md).*

**Decision**
A choice made by the user with or without AI assistance. MR:EGO's Decision Support philosophy ensures users have the information they need to make informed decisions while retaining full autonomy. *See [Product-Constitution.md](Product-Constitution.md) — Decision Support Rules.*

**Design Language**
The vocabulary of visual elements — color, typography, spacing, iconography, motion — that together create MR:EGO's recognizable visual identity. Developed in DP-1.

**Design OS (Design Operating System)**
The complete system of documents, principles, rules, patterns, and specifications that govern all design decisions in MR:EGO. Established in DP-0 and evolved through all subsequent phases.

**Design System**
The implementable toolkit of components, patterns, tokens, and guidelines that operationalizes the Design Language. Developed in DP-2.

**Documents Module**
A module for creating, managing, and exporting professional documents (resumes, cover letters, portfolios) with AI-powered assistance. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md) — Phase 7.*

## E

**Ecosystem**
The full network of modules, integrations, third-party extensions, and community contributions that extend MR:EGO's capabilities beyond the core platform.

**Enterprise Module**
Organization-scale features including team management, admin console, compliance, and analytics. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md) — Phase 11.*

**Error State**
The interface state displayed when an operation fails. Must include a clear explanation of what happened, why it happened, and what the user can do next. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 15.*

**Execution**
The automated phase of an AI-assisted workflow where approved actions are carried out. The "E" in the Guardian-Provider-Execution-Agent model. Always reversible where possible. *See [Project-Constitution.md](Project-Constitution.md) — Human Approval Philosophy.*

**Expansion**
The addition of new capabilities to MR:EGO through modular development. Governed by expansion rules in the Product Constitution. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md).*

**Experience**
A structured record of a professional engagement — including employment positions, project contributions, educational periods, volunteer work, or any activity that contributes to a user's professional narrative. Experiences are the core building blocks of a user's Career Timeline and are enriched with skills, achievements, and AI-derived insights. *See [Glossary.md](Glossary.md) — Career.*

## F

**Feature**
A distinct capability within a module that solves a specific user problem. Must pass the Feature Qualification Criteria defined in the Product Constitution.

**Future-Proofing**
The practice of making design and architecture decisions that remain valid as requirements evolve. Core to the Design OS philosophy. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md) — Future-Proofing Principles.*

## G

**Glossary**
This document. The definitive reference for MR:EGO terminology.

**Guardian**
The AI role responsible for monitoring context, identifying patterns, and surfacing insights without intrusion. The "G" in the Guardian-Provider-Execution-Agent model. *See [Project-Constitution.md](Project-Constitution.md) — AI Philosophy.*

## H

**Human Approval**
The principle that every consequential action requires explicit human consent before execution. Non-negotiable. *See [Project-Constitution.md](Project-Constitution.md) — Human Approval Philosophy.*

## I

**Identity**
The user's account, authentication, profile, and preferences across all modules. Managed by the core platform.

**Integration**
A connection between MR:EGO and an external service, enabling data exchange and cross-platform workflows.

## K

**Keyboard Navigation**
The ability to operate all interactive elements using only a keyboard. Required by WCAG and MR:EGO UX rules. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 7.*

**Knowledge**
Structured and unstructured information collected, generated, and organized by the platform, including AI-derived insights, user notes, document content, and learned patterns.

## L

**Learning Module**
A module for identifying skill gaps, recommending learning resources, and tracking professional development. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md) — Phase 3.*

## M

**Marketplace**
A platform for professional services including coaching, consulting, and skill verification. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md) — Phase 10.*

**Module**
A self-contained, independently deployable capability area within MR:EGO. Modules share core platform services but have independent lifecycles, data stores, and interfaces. *See [Product-Constitution.md](Product-Constitution.md) — Modularity Rules.*

**Module Registry**
The central system for discovering, installing, updating, and removing modules.

## N

**Navigation**
The system by which users move between screens, sections, and modules. Includes global navigation, breadcrumbs, search, and command palette. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 10.*

## O

**Onboarding**
The process by which new users become productive on the platform. Contextual, interactive, and respectful of the user's time. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 20.*

**Open API**
Public, documented, versioned API interfaces enabling third-party development on the MR:EGO platform. *See [Future-Expansion.md](Future-Expansion.md) — Phase 13.*

## P

**Personalization**
The adaptation of the platform to individual user preferences, habits, and needs. Always user-controllable and privacy-respecting. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 17.*

**Provider**
The AI role responsible for offering recommendations, generating options, and forecasting outcomes. The "P" in the Guardian-Provider-Execution-Agent model. *See [Project-Constitution.md](Project-Constitution.md) — AI Philosophy.*

## R

**Responsive Design**
Design approach ensuring interfaces work optimally across all device sizes. Mobile-first, desktop-enhanced. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 8.*

## S

**Scalability**
The capacity of the platform to grow in users, features, data volume, and geographic reach without architectural change. A core design requirement. *See [Product-Constitution.md](Product-Constitution.md) — Scalability Rules.*

**Search**
The system for finding content, features, modules, and actions across the platform. Global, fuzzy, and context-aware. *See [UX-Constitution.md](UX-Constitution.md) Rule 16.*

## T

**Token (Design Token)**
A named, reusable value representing a design decision (color, spacing, font size, shadow, etc.). The atomic unit of the Design System, enabling consistent theming and cross-platform implementation.

## U

**User Journey**
The complete lifecycle of a user's relationship with MR:EGO, from discovery through advocacy. Designed as a continuous progression, not a funnel. *See [Product-Constitution.md](Product-Constitution.md) — User Journey Philosophy.*

## W

**Workflow**
A sequence of steps the user performs to accomplish a goal. Workflows are guided, predictable, and reversible.

**Workspace**
The user's primary environment within MR:EGO, providing access to all enabled modules, data, and settings. The central hub of the platform experience.

---

*This glossary is a living document. Terms are added as the platform evolves. All documentation, design specifications, and codebases should use terminology consistent with this glossary. Refer to [Project-Constitution.md](Project-Constitution.md) for foundational concepts, [Product-Constitution.md](Product-Constitution.md) for product terminology, and [Architecture-Overview.md](Architecture-Overview.md) for architectural terms.*
